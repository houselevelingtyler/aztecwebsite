What is a cryptocurrency?

A cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security. Cryptography is a technique for secure communication that hides the meaning of a message by encoding it in a way that only the intended recipient can read. Cryptocurrencies are decentralized, meaning that they are not governed by a central authority such as a government or financial institution. Instead, transactions are recorded on a blockchain, which is a public ledger that is maintained by a network of computers. The most well-known cryptocurrency is Bitcoin, but there are many others, such as Ethereum, and Aztec Cryptocurrency.

What is a Liquidity Pool

A liquidity pool is a pool of funds locked in a smart contract that enables decentralized trading on a blockchain platform. It allows users to provide liquidity by depositing their tokens into the pool, which are then used to facilitate trades and earn fees.

What is profit sharing?

Profit sharing in crypto refers to the practice of distributing a portion of the profits generated by a cryptocurrency project to its stakeholders. This distribution typically occurs through mechanisms like token rewards or dividend payments. Profit sharing can incentivize token holders to support and participate in the project’s growth and success, aligning their interests with the long-term viability of the ecosystem.
